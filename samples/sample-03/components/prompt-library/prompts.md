# Prompt Library — Content Engine

> Four reusable prompts for the FieldPro content engine. Each is copy-paste ready. The only thing you fill in is the obvious `[PASTE BRIEF HERE]` / `[PASTE CONTENT HERE]` marker. Run each in your **FieldPro Content Engine** Project so Claude already knows the brand voice.

**How to save and reuse these:** keep this file as your master copy. Inside Claude.ai, open your **FieldPro Content Engine** Project and run each prompt in its own chat (rename the chat to the prompt's name so you can find it again). To reuse a prompt later, paste it again from this file, or pin/star the chat. If your plan shows a saved-prompt feature in the Project, paste these in there once and trigger them by name.

## Prompt 1: Long-Form Draft

**Name:** Draft Blog Post

**When to use:** Right after the Cowork research agent gives you a brief.

**Prompt:**
```
Using the research brief below, write a blog article for FieldPro's audience of
small trades-business owners.

---
[PASTE BRIEF HERE]
---

Requirements:
- Length: 900–1,100 words.
- Tone: plain, practical, encouraging — like an experienced tradesperson
  explaining it to a friend. No corporate jargon.
- Structure: a short hook intro, then one H2 section per outline item from the
  brief, then a closing CTA inviting the reader to try FieldPro's reminder and
  scheduling tools.
- Include: at least two concrete, real-world examples (e.g. a plumber, an HVAC
  crew) and one short bulleted list of action steps the reader can do this week.
- Label any numbers as illustrative estimates, not verified statistics.
- Write in British or American English consistently; do not invent fake studies.
```

## Prompt 2: LinkedIn Repurpose

**Name:** Repurpose to LinkedIn

**When to use:** After the long-form draft is approved.

**Prompt:**
```
Turn the article below into a single LinkedIn post for FieldPro.

---
[PASTE CONTENT HERE]
---

Requirements:
- Max 1,300 characters total (count them).
- First line is a scroll-stopping hook — a bold claim or a sharp question. No
  "I'm excited to share".
- 3–5 short paragraphs, one idea each, with line breaks between them so it's easy
  to skim on a phone.
- One soft CTA on the last line inviting trades owners to comment or learn more.
- Plain language, no emojis except at most one. End with up to 3 relevant
  hashtags (e.g. #Trades #SmallBusiness #FieldService).
```

## Prompt 3: Email Snippet

**Name:** Repurpose to Email

**When to use:** After the long-form draft is approved.

**Prompt:**
```
Turn the article below into a short newsletter email for FieldPro subscribers.

---
[PASTE CONTENT HERE]
---

Produce, clearly labeled:
- Subject line: under 50 characters, curiosity or benefit driven.
- Preview text: under 90 characters, complements the subject (don't repeat it).
- Body: about 150 words in 2–3 short paragraphs that tease the single most useful
  takeaway, then a one-line CTA with link text "Read the full guide →".
- Tone: friendly and direct, written to one busy owner, not a crowd.
```

## Prompt 4: Short-Form / Thread

**Name:** Repurpose to Thread

**When to use:** After the long-form draft is approved.

**Prompt:**
```
Turn the article below into a 5-tweet thread for X (Twitter) for FieldPro.

---
[PASTE CONTENT HERE]
---

Requirements:
- Exactly 5 tweets, numbered 1/5 to 5/5.
- Each tweet is 280 characters or fewer (count them; show the count in
  parentheses after each tweet).
- Tweet 1: a strong hook / claim that makes a trades owner stop scrolling.
- Tweets 2–4: one practical point each, drawn from the article.
- Tweet 5: a clear CTA pointing to the full guide.
- Plain language, no hashtags except at most one in the final tweet.
```
